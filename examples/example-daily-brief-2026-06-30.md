# Daily Market Brief - 2026-06-30

> KISS version: plain English, less Bloomberg noise, only what is worth checking.

## Bottom line

- Strategy may sell Bitcoin after years as a major buyer; that weakens an important source of demand.
- Gasoline stayed expensive even as crude fell because refining and shipping remained constrained.
- Chip stocks led a broad rise, but one good market day does not prove a lasting trend.

## What happened?

- **Strategy may switch from Bitcoin buyer to seller:** Strategy said it could sell more than $1 billion of Bitcoin to fund reserves and other obligations. **So what:** Its old loop—issue highly valued shares, buy Bitcoin, repeat—works poorly when the company trades below the value of its Bitcoin.
- **Cheap crude did not mean cheap gasoline:** WTI fell below $70, but gasoline remained high as Middle Eastern refining and Hormuz shipping stayed constrained. **So what:** Fuel inflation can persist until usable products move again, regardless of the crude headline.
- **Chips carried the stock rally:** The S&P 500 rose about 0.7%, the Nasdaq 1.4%, and the semiconductor index 3.3%. **So what:** The move was concentrated enough that investors should watch whether other sectors join before treating it as lasting.

## Learn one thing

- **Concept:** Crude oil is not gasoline.
- **Meaning:** Gasoline prices depend on refining and transport, so pump prices can stay high even when crude falls.
- **Today:** WTI fell below $70 while gasoline remained expensive because refining output and Hormuz product shipments lagged demand.
